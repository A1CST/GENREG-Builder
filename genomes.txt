# WordPipe Genome Roadmap

## Sub-label key
Every genome below is tagged **[structural]** or **[semantic]**. Structural genomes
constrain FORM (grammar, position, rhythm) using class/function features — they never
touch word meaning. Semantic genomes constrain MEANING using the distributional word-
feature spaces built from corpus statistics (24d novel-corpus SVD for the in-pipeline
genomes; 128d Wikipedia SVD + coordination-SVD for the standalone relation genomes) —
per the thesis (memory `ga-abstraction-thesis`): build the space from corpus stats,
evolution learns one relation inside it.

## Semantic-relation genomes (standalone, Wikipedia-trained, wired 2026-07-07)

Decompose "meaning" into one tiny bilinear/unary genome per relation, each over a
pre-built distributional space (see sub-label key above). All **[semantic]**. Verdict
is always the PROBES (clean hand-picked pairs), not the mined val-acc — mining off
Hearst/coordination/quote patterns is inherently noisy.

### Battery note (2026-07-07, second semantic pass)

Full probe results logged in `corpora/wikipedia/build/{hypernym,synant,synant_unified,batch2,register}.log`.

**Synonym/antonym reframe.** Training them as two separate "related vs unrelated"
detectors failed the decisive test: the separate synonym genome ranked the unrelated
control pair `dog/car` (2.61) ABOVE real synonym pairs like `quick/fast` (0.63) - it
was really just detecting relatedness, not synonymy. The separate antonym genome
failed on its OWN flagship example: `hot/cold` scored -0.29 (should be strongly
positive). Reframed per the user's suggestion: ONE genome, no unrelated pairs at all,
whose only job is "given a pair already known to be related, is it same-meaning or
opposite-meaning?" (`synant_unified.py`, bilinear over paradigmatic+coordination-SVD
concat, trained purely on the syn_pos/ant_pos boundary). Result: real improvement -
`hot/cold` flipped to +0.86 (correct), 11/14 probes correct overall (7/8 antonym, 4/6
synonym), val_acc 0.77. Residual failure: `big/large` and `small/little` (size-adjective
synonym pairs) still misclassify as antonym-leaning - likely because "big and small",
"large and small" genuinely co-occur in coordination contexts too ("big and small
businesses"), contaminating the exact signal (coordination-SVD) the reframe leans on to
separate the classes. PARTIAL, not shipped - the residual is a real, specific failure
mode, not noise.

**Sentiment (monolithic) - CUT.** Seed-word nearest-neighbor propagation drifted onto
generic high-frequency words instead of true polarity (propagated "pos" sample: first,
new, one, other, more... - not sentiment-charged at all). Probes came back inverted:
`war` scored +7.85 (should be strongly negative), `joy` scored -7.38 (should be
strongly positive). Not a marginal miss - the mining recipe itself was wrong.

**Polysemy - CUT.** val_acc looked strong (0.88) but that measured the training
distribution, not the target concept. Probes on classic polysemous words (bank, spring,
bat, light, star, book) all scored NEGATIVE (should score high). Diagnosis: the
NN-similarity-spread proxy assumes a polysemous word's neighbors scatter across its
several senses, but Wikipedia's register skews these words hard to ONE dominant sense
(e.g. "bank" mostly finance) - so the neighbor cluster stays tight and the proxy reads
monosemous. The proxy doesn't measure what it was built to measure.

**Register - CUT.** Quote-span mining (dialogue vs narration in the novel corpus)
conflated "words common in narration ABOUT feelings" with real dialogue markers.
val_acc plateaued at a weak 0.61 and probes were incoherent (`shall`/`which` scored
informal-leaning, `sir`/`nevertheless` scored formal-negative - opposite of expected).

**Analogy - CUT AS DESIGNED, roadmap note (layer-3 insight).** Chance-level accuracy
(0.48-0.53) through 600+ generations isn't a failed mining attempt to iterate on - it's
structural. "A:B::C:D" isn't a single relation living in the same distributional space
as hypernym/synonym/meronym; it's a relationship BETWEEN relationships - do pair (A,B)
and pair (C,D) instantiate the SAME relation? That's a question about the outputs of
the layer-2 relation genomes, not a question askable from raw SVD offset vectors.
Training it directly on `feat(B)-feat(A)` (what `semantic_batch2.py` did) asks a
layer-3 question with layer-2 machinery - the chance result is exactly what that
mismatch predicts. Correct future recipe: score(a1,b1,a2,b2) = agreement between
`hypernym_genome(a1,b1)` and `hypernym_genome(a2,b2)` (hypernym is the one VALIDATED
layer-2 genome to build this on first) - a small genome over relation-genome OUTPUTS,
not corpus features. Not attempted this session; logged so it isn't retried the same
broken way.

**Next concrete step - decompose sentiment instead of retrying it monolithic.** Same
discipline that built the working pipeline: if the question is too hard, make it
smaller. Instead of one "positive or negative?" genome (CUT above), split it into
several tighter binary genomes, each with its own cleaner corpus signal:
  - **Good** - does this word describe something good? (cluster: excellent, wonderful,
    pleased...)
  - **Bad** - does this word describe something bad? (cluster: terrible, awful, angry...)
  - **Intensity** - how strong/extreme is this word? (mild vs extreme, independent of
    polarity - "warm" vs "scorching", "annoyed" vs "furious")
  - **Emotion** - does this word describe an emotional state at all, vs a neutral
    fact/object? (a gate before polarity even applies)
  Each is a tighter binary question than "positive or negative" and should mine a
  cleaner seed-propagation neighborhood than the failed monolithic attempt. NOT YET
  BUILT - proposed next, pending the user's go-ahead before spinning up more training.

corrupted class triples) but is REDUNDANT with the Order genome and cannot help. Measured over
60 samples: baseline skeleton already emits only ~0.2% rare class-trigrams (Order with C=4 context
