Space Cousin Finder

Compares lens behavior across rotations. A 6×6×6 grid of deterministic lens programs (composed activations with index-derived scale/bias) is rotated about Y; where a lens lands on another grid cell, the two response signatures are correlated. Pairs above the threshold are cousins — behaviorally redundant views, the same redundancy the rotation probe found in the real radial map.

Every lens is a1(a2(scale·x+bias)), so it has two parents: the outer activation a1 and the inner a2. Lenses sharing a parent are siblings. The lineage table asks whether ancestry predicts behavior — does a shared tanh/sin parent make lenses correlate more than the population baseline — and whether the rotation-cousins are disproportionately relatives.

Redundancy = share of the bank you could drop and keep one member of each family. Selecting a parent in the lineage table rings its family members in cyan on both grids; Δ vs base is how much more (green) or less (red) siblings correlate than two random lenses. Nothing runs on load — each "find" press is a recorded run: it lands on the Runs page under Demo_Radial, and its full report downloads as JSON. Generator updated per the independent validation report: the schematic's arithmetic repeated every 140 indices (76 of 216 programs were exact duplicates, and the inner parent only took 2 of 14 values), so its cousins were mostly periodicity. Lens parameters are now hash-mixed from the index — still perfectly deterministic, but all 216 programs are distinct — and trivial self-maps no longer count as cousin pairs.
