Genome stack

The previous /evolang pipeline is archived (see docs → WORDPIPE_FIELD_NOTES). This is the rebuild, starting over with nothing carried forward except the datasets. Two groups so far, both recognition only — no generation, no grammar, no word selection:

punctuation — 5 binary genomes reading the words BEFORE a mark to recognize its intent. A single 6-way genome collapsed to guessing mostly two classes; splitting into specialists with one clean survival condition each fixed that.

opener — the mirror image: 2 binary genomes reading ONLY a sentence's FIRST word ("who/what/where/how/can/don't...") to confirm what intent the sentence is headed for.

length — given a partial sentence, is it already complete or does it need to keep growing? No fixed target length — this decision IS the length.

fill — given the words AROUND a blank (left + right), does a candidate word fit? Bidirectional, contrastive. Trained and shown for comparison, but superseded for actual generation by next.

next — the intent-conditioned successor to fill: given ONLY the words BEFORE this one plus the sentence's target end-mark, does a candidate word fit? Properly autoregressive (matches how generation actually runs) and intent-aware (a signal fill never had). length + next compose into generation below — hangman-style: grow/stop is confidence-driven, word choice is confidence-scored, nothing is fixed upfront except the intent.
